## Abstract

Blind grasping with a dexterous hand is a crucial manipulation capability. Nevertheless, learning such tactile-only policies for real robots remains challenging due to the tactile sim-to-real gap and the limited expressiveness of sparse tactile signals.

To bridge this gap, we propose a framework for tactile-only blind grasping that is deployable on a physical multi-fingered robotic hand. Our approach combines three key components. First, we introduce a **Real2Sim tactile calibration pipeline** that constructs a contact-calibrated digital-twin simulator capable of reproducing real tactile signals. Second, we improve the expressiveness of sparse tactile observations using a **layout-aware tactile encoder**, which incorporates sensor-geometry priors through self-supervised pretraining. Third, to improve generalization to unseen objects, we train object-specific reinforcement-learning experts in the calibrated simulator and aggregate their successful grasp trajectories into a **tactile-conditioned Diffusion Policy**.

We evaluate our method on a physical LEAP Hand equipped with distributed tactile sensing across 10 seen and 10 unseen objects. The deployed policy achieves a 27% real-world grasp success rate across all 20 objects, without real-world grasping demonstrations or visual input. Simulation ablations show that layout-aware tactile pretraining improves grasping performance, while sensing-level evaluations confirm that Real2Sim calibration increases the consistency of tactile contact events between simulation and hardware.

Together, these results suggest that contact-event calibration, geometry-aware tactile representation learning, and diffusion-based policy aggregation provide an effective path toward tactile-only blind grasping on real dexterous robotic hands.

## Method Overview

Our proposed Real2Sim2Real framework consists of three stages: (1) constructing a paired simulator calibrated to match real tactile contact events, (2) pretraining a layout-aware tactile encoder using privileged geometric supervision, and (3) generating simulated grasping demonstrations and training a tactile-conditioned Diffusion Policy for zero-shot real-world deployment.

### Hand-Centric Sparse Tactile Representation

We represent tactile feedback in a hand-centric 3D space. Each tactile channel $i$ is associated with a fixed sensing location $r_i \in \mathbb{R}^3$ in the local coordinate frame of its corresponding hand link. Using forward kinematics, we transform each sensing location into a common hand-centric frame:

$$x_{i,t} = T_{\ell_i}(q_t) r_i$$

The tactile observation at time $t$ is represented as a sparse hand-centric tactile point set $\mathcal{P}_t = \{p_{i,t}\}_{i=1}^{N}$, where $p_{i,t} = [x_{i,t}, c_{i,t}] \in \mathbb{R}^4$ combines the 3D sensing location with its corresponding contact feature.

### Real2Sim Tactile Calibration

We calibrate the simulator at the level of binary tactile events by collecting task-agnostic controlled-contact motions on the physical hand (tapping and sliding against a known surface). The calibration parameters $\theta = (\lambda_{\mathrm{con}}, \alpha, \lambda_{\mathrm{pre}})$ are estimated by minimizing the $\ell_1$ mismatch between real and simulated binary event sequences:

$$\theta^\star = \arg\min_{\theta} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \| \mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{sim}}_{t}(\theta) - \mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{real}}_{t} \|_1$$

### Privileged Tactile Representation Learning

We pretrain a temporal Transformer-based tactile encoder $g_{\psi}$ using privileged simulator labels unavailable on the real robot, including object pose, object geometry, and contact annotations. After pretraining, the decoder is discarded, and only the encoder is used for policy learning and deployment.

### Tactile-Conditioned Diffusion Policy

Blind tactile grasping is inherently multimodal in action distribution. We model this with a tactile-conditioned Diffusion Policy. At each control step, the pretrained tactile encoder maps the spatially grounded tactile history to a feature $z_t$, which conditions the policy to predict a future action chunk $A_t = [a_t, a_{t+1}, \ldots, a_{t+T_p-1}]$. The policy is trained exclusively on simulated expert trajectories from object-specific RL experts.

## Hardware Setup

Our physical platform consists of a 6-DoF xArm6 manipulator equipped with a 16-DoF LEAP Hand. The hand is instrumented with distributed tactile sensing: four custom-built TwinTac sensors on the fingertips (providing $4 \times 8 = 32$ binary channels) and 12 FSR channels on the palm and finger surfaces, yielding 44 tactile channels in total.

## Experiments

We evaluate our method across 20 diverse objects (10 seen, 10 unseen) on the physical LEAP Hand. The deployed policy achieves a **27% overall grasp success rate** across all objects, without any real-world grasping demonstrations or visual input.

Key findings from ablation studies:

- **Real2Sim calibration** reduces contact onset error and improves activation F1 score, confirming that calibrated simulation better reproduces real tactile observations.
- **Privileged pretraining** of the tactile encoder improves grasping performance on both seen and unseen objects in simulation, indicating that geometric supervision helps the encoder learn more structured representations from sparse contact events.

## Conclusion

This paper presents a complete pipeline for learning and deploying a blind grasping policy on a robotic dexterous hand. Our pipeline combines a tactile-enabled dexterous hand platform, a high-fidelity tactile simulation environment calibrated via Real2Sim, and an RL expert policy with a distillation framework for Sim2Real deployment. Through experiments and ablations, we find that successful blind grasping depends critically on aligning contact-event observations between simulation and the real system, as well as incorporating a pretrained tactile encoder into the policy. By jointly leveraging these components, our system achieves a 27% grasp success rate on a large and diverse real-world object set.
